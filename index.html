About Kerala

Kerala, located in the southwestern part of India, is known for its lush landscapes and extensive coastline along the Arabian Sea. The state covers an area of approximately 38,863 square kilometers and is bordered by Karnataka to the north, Tamil Nadu to the east, and the Arabian Sea to the west. Thiruvananthapuram (Trivandrum) serves as the capital, while other major cities include Kochi (Cochin) and Kozhikode (Calicut). Kerala's population is around 35 million, with Malayalam as the official language and English widely used. Economically, Kerala is diverse, with significant contributions from agriculture, tourism, and remittances from expatriates, particularly in the Gulf countries. Major agricultural products include coconut, rubber, spices, and tea. The state's scenic backwaters, beaches, and hill stations, along with its rich cultural heritage, make it a prominent tourist destination. Kerala also boasts a well-developed IT sector, particularly in cities like Kochi and Thiruvananthapuram. Culturally, Kerala is a melting pot with a rich tradition of arts and festivals. It is famous for classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, and ritualistic art forms like Theyyam. The state celebrates various festivals such as Onam, Vishu, and Thrissur Pooram with great enthusiasm. Known for its high literacy rate and advanced healthcare system, Kerala offers a high quality of life and robust social infrastructure. The state's tropical monsoon climate contributes to its rich biodiversity, making it a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural vibrancy.
